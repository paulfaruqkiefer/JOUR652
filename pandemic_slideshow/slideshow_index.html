Signs Around Town in Greater Seattle, June 2020


Photos by Paul Kiefer


I spent the last three months of my undergraduate studies cooped up in my childhood bedroom in Seattle. Had the COVID-19 pandemic not turned the world upside down, I would have spent those months cooped up in a dorm. In either timeline, I would not have much time to appreciate spring.

The public radio internship I was counting on as the first step in my post-graduation life soon vanished. The morning after watching a grim commencement address from the comfort of my parents' couch, I filed for unemployment and set out to see the city.

I've come to regret the first three months I spent moping my way around the Northwest in the summer of 2020. Not because I did anything heinous -- I just wandered, mostly in circles. I also took these pictures.
